US Crime Data: Victim, Perpetrator, and Weapon Analysis

Summary

This Tableau dashboard visualizes violent crime data across the United States, focusing on the geographic distribution of victims and perpetrators, state-by-state comparisons, and weapon usage. Although the dataset spans 1980 to 2014, the current view highlights trends from 1980 to 1991.

The analysis shows a strong geographic correlation between high victim and perpetrator counts. California and Texas stand out as the highest-concentration states, with California showing especially large spikes in both the map views and the victim-versus-perpetrator comparison chart.

The weapon analysis indicates that handguns were the most frequently used weapon category, with handgun usage in California appearing as the dominant block in the treemap. Fire also appears as a prominent secondary weapon category.
