Enhancing the YOLOv8 Model for Realtime Object Detection

Summary

This paper focuses on improving automated content moderation by detecting harmful objects in complex digital media. The Enhanced Object Detection model identifies weapons, addictive substances, violent content, and insulting gestures to improve safety across online platforms.

The research builds on YOLOv8-m and introduces structural enhancements to the model backbone and head, including updates to cross-stage partial fusion blocks and additional convolutional layers for stronger feature extraction.

A key goal was improving performance in hard-case scenarios where harmful objects are small, camouflaged, partially obscured, or placed against ambiguous backgrounds. The modified model achieved higher mean Average Precision than standard YOLOv8 models in normal and challenging scenarios without relying on heavy ensembles or attention modules.
